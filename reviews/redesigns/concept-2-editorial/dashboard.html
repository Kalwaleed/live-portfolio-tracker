Memorandum

A book that is two stocks pretending to be a portfolio.

— The Analyst

The figures are flattering. They should also be unsettling. A net liquidation of $24.71M is a function of two convictions, not twelve: NVDA at 25.6% of weight and BTC at 14.0%. Together they have produced more than four-fifths of unrealised gain this cycle. Every other position is, by the cold arithmetic, decoration.

This is not a criticism. Conviction concentrated is conviction expressed. It is, however, an instruction: the next quarter's volatility belongs almost entirely to two assets, and the rest of the book will not save you if either turns. NVDA, in particular, now exceeds the 20% house limit by 562 basis points.

Risk — concentrated & correlated

Technology weight stands at 37.4%, against a sector cap of 35%. Cross-correlations between NVDA, AVGO and MSFT have tightened to 0.78 over the trailing 90 sessions. Read literally: in a tech drawdown, the book has fewer shock absorbers than the position count suggests.

The portfolio looks diversified. It is not. It is a leveraged bet on AI compute, with an honest hedge in Saudi banking and a small altar of digital gold.

Performance — honest accounting

Lifetime return of +24.51% trails a hypothetical 60/30/10 reference (US tech / global equities / BTC) by roughly 380 bps over the same window — chiefly the cost of being underweight semis until Q2 2025. The drag is past tense; the question is whether the current weighting earns its keep through year-end.

Position limits — one breach, one warning

NVDA above ceiling. Aramco (2222.SR) approaches the 10% non-USD soft cap at 9.55%. No mandate violation, but the Riyal cross has narrowed the hedge meaningfully since January. The desk recommends a 3.5% trim of NVDA on strength, redeployed against the cash sleeve or the watchlist's ASML entry.
